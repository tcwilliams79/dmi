About the DMI

The Distributional Misery Index (DMI) measures economic pressure across income groups by combining inflation and unemployment rates with income-specific expenditure patterns.

DMI = 2.0 × (0.5 × Inflation + 0.5 × Unemployment)

Key Insight: Lower-income quintiles often experience higher DMI values due to spending more on necessities (food, housing) which may inflate faster than discretionary items.

Data Sources: BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey (CE), CPI-U, and Current Population Survey (CPS).
